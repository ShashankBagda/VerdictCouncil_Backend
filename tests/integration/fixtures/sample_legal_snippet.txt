Under the Road Traffic Act 1961 s.65, any person who drives or attempts to drive a motor
vehicle on a road without reasonable consideration for other road users is guilty of an offence
punishable by a fine not exceeding SGD 1,000 or imprisonment not exceeding 6 months.

Section 65(b) specifically addresses improper lane changes made without signalling, and the
Traffic Police enforce this provision via demerit points under Schedule 9 of the same Act.

A driver who changes lane abruptly at a junction, causing another vehicle to brake suddenly,
may be charged under s.65 and face 4 demerit points in addition to the fine.
